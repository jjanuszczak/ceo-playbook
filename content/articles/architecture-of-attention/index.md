Since the start of the year, I have maintained a weekly discipline called **[Signals]({{< relref "signals" >}})**. 

The premise is simple: for every hour spent on "slow-twitch" deep analysis, the kind found in my long-form [articles]({{< relref "articles" >}}), there are dozens of "fast-twitch" market observations that catch my eye. These are the tweets, book highlights, and technical breakthroughs that act as the early-warning system for strategic shifts.

I started the Signals series for two reasons. First, as an intellectual exercise in **information synthesis**. By forcing myself to articulate "why it matters" for every bookmark, I transform a passive information diet into an active strategic map. Second, it serves as a **discovery engine** for this platform. The Signals act as the connective tissue between the high-level strategy I provide in my [advisory practice]({{< relref "advisory" >}}) and the technical experiments and deeper dives I sometimes share in my "[engine room]({{< relref "lab" >}})".

However, after 19 consecutive weeks of documentation, the individual signals have begun to coalesce into a broader narrative. By stepping back and auditing these observations from January through May 2026, a clear evolution of focus has emerged. 

What started as a search for raw AI capability has matured into an investigation of organizational culture and systemic infrastructure. Here is what 19 weeks of market "twitch" reveals about the strategic direction of the rest of the year.

## The Evolution of Attention: From Capability to Culture

The most significant evolution in my writing has been the **shifting "altitude" of AI analysis**.

*   **Early Q1 (The Search for Capability):** The focus was on mapping the limits of LLMs—distinguishing between "reasoning" and "reaction", and identifying the need for hardened implementation over exuberant exploration.
*   **Mid Q1 (The Engineering Discipline):** Attention moved to the professionalization of the era. I began discussing "Agentic Engineering" and the concept of *SaaSmageddon*, arguing that traditional SaaS UIs were becoming a "tax" on efficiency that autonomous agents would eventually bypass.
*   **Early Q2 (The Economic Realignment):** Focus shifted to the "Structural Jevons Paradox" and the idea that as the unit cost of intelligence falls, the total demand for systemic complexity will explode.
*   **The Present (The Cultural Moat):** We have landed on the "Human Container." Because AI capability is rapidly commoditizing, the only remaining moats are vertical mastery and the culture that directs the technology.

## What Has Changed the Most vs. Least?

**The Biggest Shift: The framing of the AI Moat.** I began 2026 looking for moats in *capability* (what models can do). I am beginning the month of May concluding that models are utilities. The moat is now found in **vertical mastery** (owning the industry-specific outcome) and **identity** (the culture of the organization).

**The Constant: Systems Thinking.** This remains the intellectual spine of the platform. Whether analyzing 18th-century maritime insurance or photonic computing, the lens remains the same: **Excellence is found in the optimization of the system, not just its parts.**

## Emerging Trends

*   **Infrastructure as Strategy:** Power is consolidating in the "plumbing", the settlement layers, data protocols, and legal codes that underpin commerce.
*   **The Agentic Pivot:** A fundamental move from "Systems of Record" (SaaS) to "Systems of Action" (Agents).
*   **Goal-Alignment as the New API:** In an autonomous era, the primary job of leadership is no longer managing process, but precisely defining and aligning *intent*.

## Predictions for the Rest of 2026

*   **The Great UI Culling:** We will see a crisis among legacy SaaS giants as "Agentic UIs" make traditional dashboards obsolete. Churn will hit those who sell tools rather than outcomes.
*   **Rise of the "System Architect" Leader:** The distinction between the CTO and COO will continue to blur. Success will belong to leaders who can "code" an organization’s operating model as rigorously as they code its software.
*   **The "AI-Only" Entity:** We are likely to witness the first "AI-Only" startup—an organization designed from Day 1 to function with zero human employees in its core operational loop, governed entirely by goal-alignment protocols.
*   **Verification as the Premium:** In an age of infinite generative content, the market premium will shift from *creation* to **High-Fidelity Verification**.

We are moving out of the "AI Model Era" and into the Systemic AI Era, where the value lies not in intelligence itself, but in the architecture that directs it.
