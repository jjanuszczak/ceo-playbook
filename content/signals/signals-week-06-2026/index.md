The theme of the week is **The Agentic Pivot**. We are witnessing a fundamental shift from SaaS as an interface to the "System of Record" to AI Agents as the "System of Action." This transition, which observers are calling *SaaSmageddon*, represents the crumbling of the traditional workflow moat. When agents can navigate UIs better than humans, the UI itself becomes a liability: a "tax" on efficiency rather than an asset. **Many abstractions become costly in light of agentic AI**. This shift necessitates a move toward "Agentic Engineering" where software is designed for autonomous execution rather than just human interaction.

*   **Summary:** Zain argues that AI agents will bypass traditional SaaS UIs, making "Systems of Record" like Salesforce or Workday mere data repositories while the "System of Action" moves to the agent layer.
*   **Why it Matters:** For decades, the interface *was* the moat. If agents are the new users, the value accrues to those who control the intent and the final action, not the interface.
*   **My Take:** **The UI is now a tax.** Companies that don't provide clean API and MCP access for agents will find themselves abstracted away by those who do.

## Longer Reads

#### [Microsoft and Software Survival](https://stratechery.com/2026/microsoft-and-software-survival/) - Ben Thompson
*   **Summary:** Thompson analyzes the tech market's reaction to AI disruption, noting that software stocks are falling as the market realizes the traditional SaaS moat is evaporating.
*   **Why it Matters:** When the most respected voice in tech strategy calls it a crisis, it's no longer a trend. It's a tectonic shift.
*   **My Take:** **The compute supply crisis is the new bottleneck.** Strategy must now focus on securing the intelligence layer, not the application layer.
